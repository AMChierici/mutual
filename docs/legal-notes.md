# Legal & Regulatory Notes

**Mutual is software. We are not lawyers. None of this is legal advice. The notes below are starting points for a real conversation with a real lawyer in your jurisdiction.**

## The line that matters

Most jurisdictions distinguish:

- **Mutual aid / gifting / savings circles** among people with a clear pre-existing relationship (family, members of a registered association, employees of a co-op). Generally lightly regulated or unregulated.
- **Insurance**, where strangers pool risk for a premium with an enforceable promise to pay claims. Heavily regulated. Requires licensing in most countries.

Mutual is designed for the first category. The moment a pool admits strangers via a public sign-up and promises payouts, you're probably operating an unlicensed insurer. Don't do that.

## Practical guardrails the software encourages

- Pools have a member cap suggested in templates (typically 25-50 for affinity groups, larger only for clearly-defined organizations)
- Membership requires endorsement from existing members in most templates
- We do not federate pools across strangers
- Documentation repeatedly reminds organizers to file real-insurance-covered claims with their real insurer

## Jurisdictions worth checking

- **US:** state-by-state insurance regulation. Some states explicitly carve out "mutual benefit" or "fraternal" associations under certain conditions.
- **UK:** the Friendly Societies Act 1992 still exists and governs exactly this kind of structure. Check whether your pool needs to register.
- **EU:** Solvency II generally exempts very small mutuals, but member-state rules vary. Germany's VAG, France's Code de la mutualité, etc.
- **Canada:** provincial regulation, generally similar to the US pattern.

## What you should do

1. Read your jurisdiction's actual rules.
2. If your pool is anything bigger than a household or a tight-knit group of friends/colleagues, talk to a lawyer before launching.
3. Keep clear records (which Mutual helps with) — most regulatory ambiguity gets worse, not better, when records are messy.
4. If a regulator asks, the honest answer is "we run a savings and mutual-aid pool among [defined affinity], we don't sell coverage to the public." Make sure that's actually true.
