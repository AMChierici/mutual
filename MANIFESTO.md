# Manifesto

Insurance, as a financial product, works. Insurance, as an industry, has drifted a long way from the people it serves.

Premiums rise without explanation. Claims are denied by adjusters paid to deny them. Policies are written to be unreadable. The relationship between the insured and the insurer is adversarial by design — every dollar paid out is a dollar lost from someone's quarterly number.

This is not how humans have always pooled risk.

Long before joint-stock insurers, there were friendly societies in industrial Britain, hui in China, susu in West Africa, tanda in Mexico, takaful across the Muslim world, and a thousand other names for the same idea: a defined group of people who know each other, contribute regularly, and pay each other out when life goes sideways. The math was rougher. The trust was real. The surplus stayed in the community.

These structures still exist. Two billion people use them. They run on cash boxes, group chats, and spreadsheets that get corrupted every other year.

**Mutual is software for that.**

We are not trying to replace your home insurance. We are not building a token. We are not promising disruption. We are writing a clean, auditable, self-hostable tool for the kind of pooling that already works, and making it boring enough that an actuary, a co-op treasurer, and a parent can all trust it.

If you are an actuary who has ever wondered what your skills could do outside a reinsurance tower, we want your pull requests.

If you are a developer who has ever filed a claim and felt like a number, we want your pull requests.

If you are a family, a climbing gym, a freelancer collective, a group of dog owners, a worker co-op — try it. Tell us where it breaks.

The goal is not to bring down insurance. The goal is to make the alternative real enough that insurance has to compete on something other than inertia.

Small pools. Open code. Honest math.
